What is ADHD?

Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) is a neurodevelopmental condition that affects how the brain manages attention, focus, and impulses. It can influence everyday life at work, in relationships, and personal wellbeing. Many people only discover ADHD as adults.

Inattention

  • Difficulty sustaining focus
  • Disorganisation and forgetfulness
  • Starting tasks but struggling to finish
  • Distracted by external events or thoughts

Hyperactivity

  • Restlessness and “on the go” energy
  • Fidgeting or frequent movement
  • Finding quiet activities difficult
  • Preferring fast-paced environments

Impulsivity

  • Acting before thinking
  • Interrupting conversations
  • Impatience in day-to-day situations
  • Difficulty waiting your turn

Types of ADHD

Predominantly inattentive: focus, attention, organisation.

Predominantly hyperactive-impulsive: restlessness, impulsivity.

Combined type: mix of inattention and hyperactivity/impulsivity.

Strengths & support

ADHD can bring challenges and strengths (creativity, energy, problem-solving). Support may include psychoeducation, therapy, practical strategies, coaching-informed approaches, and collaboration with medical professionals when appropriate.
